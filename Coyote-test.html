Coyote

The coyote (Canis latrans) is a species of canine native to North America. It is smaller than its close relative, the gray wolf, and slightly smaller than the closely related eastern wolf and red wolf. It fills much of the same ecological niche as the golden jackal does in Eurasia. The coyote is larger and was once referred to as the American jackal by behavioral ecologists. Other historical names for the species include the prairie wolf and the brush wolf.

The coyote is listed as least concern by the International Union for Conservation of Nature, due to its wide distribution and abundance throughout North America. The species is versatile, able to adapt to and expand into environments modified by humans; urban coyotes are common in many cities.

Coyotes of the Southwest

  • Mearns' coyote(C. l. mearnsi)
  • Texas plains coyote(C. l. texensis)
  • San Pedro Martir coyote(C. l. clepticus)

The coyote has 19 recognized subspecies which can be found throughout its extensive range which reaches from Alaska through the majority of North America and down to southern Mexico and Central America.

Features

Coyote males average 18 to 44 lb in weight, while females average 15 to 40 lb, though size varies geographically. Northern subspecies, which average 40 lb, tend to grow larger than the southern subspecies of Mexico, which average 25 lb. Total length ranges on average from 3 ft 3 in to 4 ft 5 in; comprising a tail length of 16 in, with females being shorter in both body length and height.

Diet

Typical Diet of the Coyote

  1. Deer
  2. Birds
  3. Reptiles
  4. Fish
  5. Rabbits

Along with the above examples, the coyote has also been witnessed consuming bison in the form of carrion as well as the occasional fruit or vegetable.

Predators

With the exception of areas where their territory overlaps with gray wolves and cougars, predation on adult coyotes is relatively rare but multiple other predators can be occasional threats. In some cases, adult coyotes have been preyed upon by both American black and grizzly bears, American alligators, large Canada lynx, and golden eagles.
